UAE are capable of causing a few jitters in the opposition as we saw in the World Cup last year. A new day and a different opponent must boost their confidence. If they play their cards right, bat sensibly and bowl a tight line and length, the hosts could experience something different. Toss and teams coming up in a short while....Bangladesh have lost three of their last four T20 matches and will be under a lot of pressure to deliver in front of the home crowd. The hosts did not look convincing against India but should find their footing against the Asian minnows. On a pitch that provided plenty of help to the bowlers, the Tigers allowed MS Dhoni's men to register a productive total of 166 in the competition's opening clash. Despite Sabbir Rahman's 32-ball 44, they never had a chance to come back in the game after losing wickets at regular intervals, managing 121 for seven from their 20 overs. Bangladesh are yet to substantiate themselves in the T20 design but one can expect a strong execution from a youthful, eager group.Hello and welcome to the 3rd encounter of the Asia Cup between Bangladesh and UAE. Both teams will look to change their fortunes after faltering in their respective opening fixtures. Of course, the relatively experienced hosts will start as favourites, but as evidenced by their plucky performance against Sri Lanka, UAE seem capable of springing a surprise. The bowling from both sides look good, with seamers and the spinners doing their job well. It is the batting which needs to be addressed as neither side showed any application in their opening match.Preview by Ganesh ChandrasekaranThe performance against Sri Lanka was a shot in the arm for UAE. Their bowling came up trumps against a quality batting unit like Sri Lanka and with a little more application from the batsmen, they could have well caused an upset. But Malinga's brilliance came in the way. What the game has done, however, is spice things up forUAE's second consecutive encounter - against Bangladesh on Friday (February 26).The hosts themselves will be eager to stage a winning comeback having lost their first game against India. A full house is expected and will be one of the factors to counter for an inexperienced team like UAE. Says Amjad Javed, the skipper "I think they have played two games on TV. Definitely, tomorrow there will be a crowd supporting Bangladesh but if you don't see outside, stick to your task and concentrate on the game, (it will be) good for UAE. I want my boys to do that only. Not seeing the crowd outside, but seeing the ball is most important for us."For UAE, the pressure of playing in front of big crowds and in front of a larger television audience is as much a factor to counter than playing just the opposition.Javed insists that his team is out there to win and not just compete. "If you are being negative with your team then there is really no use of coming and playing," he chuckles. "My statement was very simple- just go out there and win the game."In that aspect, the performance against Sri Lanka has only raised the confidence of the team to do even better. Their bowling department restricted Sri Lanka to just 129 but when the final nails had to be hammered in, the batsmen fell short. "If the batsmen see the bowlers restricting a team like Sri Lanka to under 130, they will want to do well. I think they will do well tomorrow," exudes a positive Javed.Bangladesh though will be a different kettle of fish. What was evident in the first two games of the Asia Cup is that the grass is greener on the side of the pacers.In both games, the spinners have been pushed to the backseat quite unlike traditional sub-continental conditions. The hosts boast of a pace quartet with plenty of talent, led by Mashrafe Mortaza. Each adds a different dimension to the attack. If its control for the skipper, then it is variations for Mustafizur, pace and bounce for Taskin, swing from a slinging action for Al-Amin Hossain. They will be under the spotlight once again against UAE. With their batsmen showing a weakness against quality pace bowling, Bangladesh will be hoping to inflict similar wounds.While the bowlers have found it easier, given the assistance from the pitch, naturally it is the reverse for batsmen. Mortaza will be wary of the batting department which has let him down consistently, particularly in T20Is. In the absence of Tamim Iqbal, there is additional pressure on the rest of the batsmen to step up. They will be pondering a lot upon the batting order as well. With the likes of Mahmudullah and Mushfiqur Rahim coming quite late in the middle order, Bangladesh find themselves in a quandary when the power-hitting needs to be done right from the outset. A little tinkering might well happen to accommodate the right men at the right positions.It is unlikely that UAE will change much anything with regards to their bowlers. A few changes in the batting unit might be warranted but even that is unlikely given how Javed backed them to go for their shots again. The skipper was vehement in defence of his batsmen and insisted that the only way the runs were going to come was by playing those shots.With both teams battling similar problems within their ranks, the contest ought to be a good one. Javed was confident of UAE pulling off a few upsets in the tournament.
