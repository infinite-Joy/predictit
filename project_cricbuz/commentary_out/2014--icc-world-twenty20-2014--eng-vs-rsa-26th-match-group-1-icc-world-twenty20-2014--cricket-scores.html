It has been an unfortunate tournament for Stuart Broad's men so far with their game against New Zealand being decided by the D/L method after rain interrupted.South Africa captain Faf du Plessis will miss the vital clash after being banned for his side's slow over-rates, providing a timely warning for opposite number Stuart Broad. Star batsman AB de Villiers will step in for du Plessis as skipper for the game against England.Du Plessis was handed a one-game ban by the International Cricket Council after match referee David Boon ruled South Africa fell one over short in their allotted time during Thursday's narrow success over Holland - a second such offence in a 12-month period.Du Plessis, an important batsman and crack fielder as well as on-field leader, will be a big loss and raise England's hopes of staying alive in the tournament.But they must also take heed of Du Plessis' fate after Broad was also penalised by Boon.England were judged to be two overs behind the required rate in their thrilling victory over Sri Lanka but, as a first offence, Broad was handed only a fine comprising 40 per cent of his match fee.Any further transgression in the tournament would see England suffer the same punishment as Du Plessis and leave Broad to watch his team-mates from the sidelines.As it is, the 27-year-old is starting to harbour thoughts of an unlikely success at a tournament that England entered in what seemed a chronic downward spiral.Alex Hales' dashing 116 not out against Sri Lanka made him the country's first international T20 centurion and breathed unexpected life into England's challenge in Bangladesh.Now Broad is hoping to harness that energy and become challengers for the main prize."T20 is a momentum game and the innings we chased against Sri Lanka was one of the best I've ever witnessed," he said."The confidence and belief we will get from that going into our final two group games is going to be huge. It's in our hands a little bit now."If we had lost against Sri Lanka we were pretty much out of the World Cup. With that sort of pressure, it's exciting to see players come through that."We have got two group games left and we're very confident we can do well in those."We know what these tournaments are like, if you can get on a run, if you can get a few guys going you can win these tournaments and it's not out of our grasp."The only downside of Thursday's restorative six-wicket success was a series of missed chances in the field, with four dropped catches and one botched run out making life harder than it had to be.Coach Ashley Giles responded by ordering evening fielding practice in Chittagong, putting his charges through their paces under lights and in dewy conditions.Teams:England (From): Michael Lumb, Alex Hales, Moeen Ali, Eoin Morgan, Jos Buttler(w), Ravi Bopara, Stuart Broad(c), Tim Bresnan, Jade Dernbach, Chris Jordan, Ian Bell, Chris Woakes, James Tredwell, Stephen Parry, Craig KieswetterSouth Africa (From): Hashim Amla, Quinton de Kock(w), AB de Villiers(c), Jean-Paul Duminy, Farhaan Behardien, David Miller, Albie Morkel, Wayne Parnell, Dale Steyn, Morne Morkel, Imran Tahir, Lonwabo Tsotsobe, Beuran Hendricks, Aaron PhangisoLATEST NEWSZouks look to build on home momentum1h agoEx-cricketers set to benefit from reforms3h agoMathew & Co will have to move out of KCA4h agoKSCA postpones AGM, polls4h agoR Ashwin - twice the asset, twice as dangerous5h agoMore News.cb-topic-header{margin-left:0px!important;} .cb-list-heading{font-weight:normal!important} MOBILE SITE & APPSm.cricbuzz.comAndroidiOSWindows MobileBlackberryChrome ExtensionFOLLOW US ONfacebooktwittergoogle+PinterestRSS FeedCOMPANYCareersAdvertisePrivacy PolicyTerms of UseCricbuzz TV Ads© 2016 Cricbuzz.com, Times Internet Limited.
