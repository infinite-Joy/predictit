Let's take a look at how both the teams have done so far.Preview:Though a statistical mismatch, Australia's final Pool A game against Scotland at Bellerive Oval in Hobart gives them the opportunity to polish the rough edges around their campaign in the ICC Cricket World Cup 2015 so far. Still searching for their first victory in World Cups, Scotland return home irrespective of the result but will hope to put up a stronger display than they have during the course of the tournament.Venue: Bellerive Oval, HobartDate: March 14, 2015Time: 0900 Hrs ISTWeather: A maximum of 21 degrees with showers predicted in the afternoonTeam newsAustraliaDespite putting up dominant performances against England and Sri Lanka, Australia haven't looked like their usual self yet, and their defeat to New Zealand and the rained out game with Bangladesh means some of the players haven't spent enough time in the middle. Australia captain Michael Clarke has indicated that the batting order will be shuffled around to let some of his players get a decent hit in the middle, which may see the captain himself come in at No 3, especially if Scotland decide to bat first on winning the toss.Australia have already announced their XI for the game with Pat Cummins replacing left-arm spinner Xavier Doherty, who had played in the previous game against Sri Lanka in Sydney. There, however, continues to be no place for local boy George Bailey, who was left out of the side since Clarke returned to the side.Probable XI: Aaron Finch, David Warner, Michael Clarke (capt), Steven Smith, Shane Watson, James Faulkner, Glenn Maxwell, Brad Haddin, Mitchell Johnson, Mitchell Starc, Pat Cummins.ScotlandScotland came agonizingly close to recording their first-ever World Cup win against Afghanistan but with just one more game to be played and that too against Australia, the elusive first victory will have to wait. The last time they played Australia in the World Cup was way back in 1999 when the to-be champions had a bit of a stutter, and Scotland would seek inspiration from that game to instill some confidence in themselves.Despite suffering heavy defeats, some of Scotland's players have thrived on the big stage with impressive performances. Until Trent Boult took a couple of wickets against Bangladesh on Friday, Scotland medium-pacer Josh Davey was leading the bowling charts for the tournament, which they should be proud of. Kyle Coetzer has trailed off since his 156 against Bangladesh, but will look for one last flourish in this campaign.Majid Haq's controversial removal from the squad after his omission from Scotland's last game should not affect the make-up of the team, which in all likelihood will remain unchanged against Australia.Probable XI: Kyle Coetzer, Calum MacLeod, Matt Machan, Preston Mommsen (capt), Freddie Coleman, Richie Berrington, Michael Leask, Matthew Cross (wk), Rob Taylor, Josh Davey, Alasdair EvansWatch out forJames Faulkner has had a quiet World Cup so far, especially after missing the first few games with injury. He has been steady with the ball but hasn't had enough time in the middle with the bat and was run out first ball against Sri Lanka. A promotion up the order could be likely and it may just be the fillip Faulkner needs ahead of the knockout stages of the competition.After scoring a half-century in his first World Cup game, Matt Machan has failed to convert the starts he has got in the subsequent matches, and against Australia he will look to correct that. Machan has looked comfortable against both pace and spin and looked solid, but the 24-year-old will hope to impress against the toughest set of bowlers he has come up against yet.Quotes"I think we'd be silly to pick our team today for the World Cup quarter-final.
