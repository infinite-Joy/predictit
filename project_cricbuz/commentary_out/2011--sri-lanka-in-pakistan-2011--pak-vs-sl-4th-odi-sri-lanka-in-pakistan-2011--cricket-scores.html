I, Sid V, will be joined by Sid P. Stay tuned for the toss and the team news.Preview by PradeepThe series moves from Dubai to Sharjah, but there seems to be no end to the woes of Sri Lanka. They went into the third ODI with some confidence, started slowly, hit back in the middle and towards the end of the Pakistan innings, set up a reasonable chase. Despite not starting the chase brightly, they looked well in control until the mandatory batting powerplay in the 36th over. Things began to change in that little passage of play and Sri Lanka ended up losing a match that was well in their grasp.The series has now reached a tipping point for Sri Lanka. They need nothing short of two straight wins to go back home with something to show for their efforts. Pakistan have grown in confidence as the series has progressed and will quietly fancy their chances of sealing a series victory at a venue that has been a home away from home for them.Pakistan: One of the biggest positives to have emerged from the Test and ODI series is the unity of the current Pakistan team. This can be attributed to the fact that they have been led by a man who has displayed a very calm and collective mindset during times of crisis. The solidity of the opening pair is another area which had bogged down the team from a number of years. Imran Farhat and Mohammad Hafeez seem to have solved that problem at least for a certain amount of time. The solidity of the opening pair has allowed the middle order to blossom. The power-hitters deceived to flatter during the 3rd ODI, but if there is one lower middle order the bowlers across the world fear, it must be that of Pakistan.The bowling unit looks very settled. Umar Gul has lead the attack with aplomb and has been well supported by the likes of Cheema and Razzaq. Saeed Ajmal is adding to his reputation while Afridi continues to dazzle with the ball in his hand. The revelation though has been Hafeez. One expected the experienced Sri Lankan batsmen to prosper against him, but Hafeez has troubled them with clever variations. He has been the most economical of the bowlers and has wickets to boot as well.Sri Lanka: The team from the island nation looks like a ragged outfit at the moment. Their spirits were up after the 2nd ODI only to be punctured by some poor cricket in the final 15 overs of the chase in the last game. Most of the batsmen have got starts but have not converted it into something bigger. They have not been helped by some of the team management's bizarre tactics like the one in the last match where they promoted Lasith Malinga to No.7 during the batting PP. Ideally, in such a scenario one would expect a power-hitter to be up the order and the visitors did have one in the form of Thissara Perera. He was instead somewhat wasted at No. 9. Sri Lanka rely heavily on their top-order and one of them must get a big ton if they are to challenge their formidable opponents in the do or die encounter. The bowling continues to remain a problem. Perera has been their best bowler so far while their pace spearhead Malinga was off colour in the last match. With the series hanging by the thread, Sri Lanka will hope for their champion to rise up to the occasion. Dilhara Fernando has been very expensive in the matches so far while the spinners have kept the batsmen honest without troubling them in any manner.Team News: Pakistan has no major worries to think about. Abdul Razzaq seems to be the only weak link at the moment but may retain his place. For Sri Lanka, Dilhara Fernando has been very expensive and might be benched in favour of either Suraj Randiv or Chanaka Welegedara.Final Word: Pakistan has played 108 ODIs at Sharjah which is more then the number of matches they have played in Lahore, Karachi and Rawalpindi put together. They have won a whopping 74 of those and have a W/L ratio of 2.24:1.
