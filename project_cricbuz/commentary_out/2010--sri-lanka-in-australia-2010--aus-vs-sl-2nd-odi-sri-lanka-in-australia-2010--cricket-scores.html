But this has been the case with the Kangaroos in the past couple of months. Following their beating in India, the Aussies suffered losses at home in the one-off T20 and the 1st ODI against Sri Lanka.Australia:The Australians were decent with the bat during the first ODI, but failed to convert good starts into big scores. Barring Michael Hussey, who scored a gritty 71, the likes of Haddin, Clarke and Marsh began well but could not continue in the same vein for Australia to post a big score. Defending a modest total of 239, the Australians thought they had Sri Lanka on the mat when the tourists were reduced to 107/8. But a 132-run 9th wicket partnership between Angelo Mathews and Lasith Malinga ruined the Aussie party.The indifferent form of the middle order has been a concern for sometime. The home team felt the absence of Ricky Ponting, who had to miss the 1st ODI in order to attend his grand mother's funeral. While Ponting would be back to skipper the side for the 2nd game, the Australian side would be without the services of Shaun Marsh, who has been ruled of the series after injuring his lower back. Batting is the strength of the current Australian setup and the likes of Clarke, Watson, Ponting, Haddin and White have to stand up and make it count.In the bowling department, the Australians have unearthed a potential match-winner in the form of Xavier Doherty, who in his debut match against Sri Lanka came up with an impressive spell of spin bowling, picking up 4/46. The likes of Peter Siddle, Mitchell Johnson and John Hastings are also part of the ODI setup and overall, the bowling department depicts a settled unit.Sri Lanka:The Sri Lankans, unlike their previous ventures in Australia, have been on the money right from the start, holding the home team by the throat. They are now in a position to take an unassailable 2-0 lead in the three match series. However, the tourists have a lot of work at hand if they are to achieve that. While the bowling performance of the Lankans has been up to the mark in the T20 game and the 1st ODI, the same cannot be said about the batting.It has been a roller-coaster performance from the Sri Lankan top order during their endeavours in the past few months. Like the Australians, they too have not been consistent in their performances with the bat. Barring a couple of decent scores from captain Kumar Sangakkara and the remarkable fight-back by Angelo Mathews and Lasith Malinga, the others have been quite inconsistent.
