For India, they have finally come back into form on this tour and they will look to continue their good run.Preview by SrivathsaThere is a very famous adage in English which people almost wait to use in opportune moments:- 'What goes around, comes around'. In a summer where they thrashed India 4-0 in the Test series and their old war-horse Ricky Ponting stuck gold with batting form, Australia have gone from 'the celebrated' to the brink of 'the humiliated'. Their sojourn in the shorter formats of the game started with a win in the T20 and ODI tri-series against India and they even managed to beat Sri Lanka in the second ODI. But that was a couple of weeks back. In the meantime, the skipper Michael Clarke injured himself, Ponting came in to lead the side and they had to bear the brunt of a dejected coach who blasted the side for their consecutive losses. They will be looking to arrest the slide against an Indian side, which is not at it's best, but has given itself a shot in the arm with a slew of compact performances. It will be interesting to see which of the two teams will take the initiative to dominate:- Speaking with respect to behaviour, Australia are known to be aggressive no matter what, but India surely have the edge. IndiaIndia have done extremely well so far in the CB series after a disastrous test series, which saw them being humiliated. They stand at the top of the table after the halfway stage of the tournament and that is due to some excellent all-round performances. The return to form of Gambhir is a very good sign and down the order, there is 'Captain Cool' - MS Dhoni who is proving himself to be the best finisher in the ODI game at this moment. Even though they have looked like the best side in the competition, India would want to finish off games more clinically. The last game against Sri Lanka is a perfect example ? they were cruising at one stage and then allowed the Lankans to come back. Good teams rarely let the opposition of the hook and that's what India have to aim to do. Suresh Raina and Rohit Sharma need some runs under their belt, especially the former who is going through a lean run at the moment.With India looking to rotate the three openers, it wouldn't be a surprise if Sachin Tendulkar is given the day off at Brisbane. If that happens, then Sehwag will come back in and he too needs to put his head down and try for a big score ? which has been eluding him this entire series. The bowling looks very balanced and with Irfan Pathan batting at 8, the depth of this Indian side is something to marvel at. India know a victory on Sunday, will take them a step closer to their first target ? which is to reach the finals. For that to happen, they need to put in a disciplined effort on a track which is expected to be pacy.AustraliaIt started so well for the hosts, but the last couple of games have seen them being very ordinary. The injury to Michael Clarke has come at the wrong time and they were humiliated by Sri Lanka in a rain affected game the other day. Australia's batting was looking very suspect before the start of the tournament and it has been proved to be their bug-bear. Ricky Ponting hasn't looked like the Ponting who made huge scores in the test series. Even a promotion as opener in one of the games didn't help. He is under huge pressure and captaincy has added to his headaches. With Clarke not available on Sunday, it is imperative for Ponting to get a big score and if he does, then that confidence will automatically filter into his captaincy which can only prove to be beneficial for Australia.Matthew Wade is another who has flopped miserably with the bat in the series, with Warner proving to be hit and miss the opening combination have failed in every game which has put the already brittle middle order under more strife. Peter Forrest who made his debut looks a very powerful player and is a player in the Shane Watson mould ? strong square of the wicket and good straight down the ground. Without Clarke's calm presence in the middle order, it is up to the likes of Mike Hussey to shore up the innings and then provide the late impetus. Clint McKay may have conceded the 13 runs in the last over to give India an unlikely victory, but he certainly has looked the best Aussie bowler on view. His back of the hand slower balls have troubled everyone and has mixed up his variations pretty well. Doherty too has applied the breaks during the middle overs and has provided a perfect partner for McKay.
