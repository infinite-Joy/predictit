Today, the hosts, Bangladesh, will also look to put up a slick performance as they aim to trip Sri Lanka and carry their winning momentum forward.Preview by Ganesh ChandrasekaranFour games into the Asia Cup 2016 and we are yet to see the big-hitters take the centre stage. This Twenty20 tournament has been a far cry from the norm in the format - not that it has failed to produce compelling cricket. One could even stretch the argument further to perhaps point out that this tournament has given the format itself a different colour. The primary reason for this has been the pitches produced. With more than a tinge of green on all the surfaces so far, teams will be revisiting their playing XI and even strategies to counter this factor.Both Bangladesh and Sri Lanka have enough resources in their ranks to counter it, in the likely situation that the conditions remain the same. Bangladesh's four pacers - Mashrafe Mortaza, Mustafizur Rahman, Taskin Ahmed and Al-Amin Hossain - are fast gaining in reputation and will be more than a handful in helpful conditions, as they showed in the game against the United Arab Emirates. Their batting has been the bigger worry with failures in first two games. One of their key members in the unit - Shakib Al Hasan - has a high score of just 20 in his last nine T20I innings. That will be the biggest worry for the skipper. Shakib's failures gain more prominence in the absence of Tamim Iqbal in the line-up. The rest of the batting unit will not find it any easier against Sri Lanka and if Bangladesh are to show any improvement in this format, it is once again Shakib's responsibility to show them the way forward. The think-tank would also be pondering the need to shuffle the batting order a touch to adapt to the conditions. Mahmudullah and Mushfiqur Rahim will probably be of more value to them higher up in the order in these conditions. The bowling unit is far more settled and it's unlikely it'll see a change for this gameSri Lanka will be sweating upon the fitness of Lasith Malinga, who presented a grim outlook after bowling in the match against UAE. Malinga was playing his first game in four months after a knee injury kept him out and did not seem to happy at the end of that game despite picking up a match-winning four-wicket haul. Sri Lanka will be treading cautiously on his fitness and availability for the rest of the tournament since their aim would be to keep Malinga fit for the World T20. Their other worry will be the batting department, which much like Bangladesh, came a cropper against UAE.That time around, the batsmen were simply not prepared for a pitch like that in Bangladesh but if faced with a similar situation, they will be prepared to counter it. Sri Lanka preferred to use Dinesh Chandimal as an opener in that game and he promptly responded with an important half-century. That knock should help him hold the spot for a longer period now. Tillakaratne Dilshan and Angelo Mathews are the other two bedrocks of the batting unit which also consists of a few explosive hitters. Their well-rounded batting department might well be the clincher against Bangladesh with both bowling units at par, given the conditions at play.It is unlikely that either team will make any changes to their playing XI unless injury-enforced. For Bangladesh, with a loss already in their first game against India, this game will be of bigger significance. Their batsmen will fancy their chances a little more against Sri Lanka than they would do so against Pakistan, given their display against India. A loss here will hamper their situation further. On the other hand, Sri Lanka will be gunning for a spot at the top of the table with a second successive win.The toss-up eventually might be between just the two bowling units of the sides. On paper, there is not much that can separate the two. Sri Lanka though go in with the edge because of a more experienced batting group. The spotlight will naturally be on the new-ball bowlers and openers of both teams. They are the ones who will set the tone, and on a greenish pitch they gain even more importance. The changes in strategy will also be tactical. There might not be a slam-bang approach from the openers.
