Sri Lanka captain Kumar Sangakkara admitted that his knowledge of Canada is only confined to the 40-year-old John Davison, who once scored the fastest World Cup hundred, a record broken by Australian Matthew Hayden four years later. However, Sri Lanka would be not bother much about this as they look to start their World Cup campaign with a rousing victory.Head to Head:The two countries have played each other only once. They faced off in the 2003 edition of the World Cup during which Sri Lanka handed a comprehensive defeat to Canada. The Canadian team was bowled out cheaply for just 36 runs at Paarl, which still remains to be the lowest total in a World Cup game.Build up to the game:Sri Lanka: The Lankans, on home-soil, are one of the most formidable sides, and the opposition would also be wary of their current form as the co-hosts look to be in great shape. In November 2010, Sri Lanka won their first ever ODI series against Australia in Australia and followed that up with a confident 2-0 win against West Indies at home a couple of weeks back. They also won their two WC warm-up matches quite comfortably. They posted a formidable 351/5 during their 156 run win against Netherlands in the first game while they chased down 282 against West Indies in their second warm-up match. The batsmen have been in fine nick with Tharanga, Dilshan, Samaraweera, Sangakkara, Kapugedara and Chamara Silva all getting decent hits out at the middle and they are likely to relish the inexperienced Canadian bowling attack. The Lankan bowlers look fit and all raring to go and the Canadian batsmen would have to watch-out for Malinga and Muralitharan, who are likely to cause the most problems.Canada: Although minnows, the Canadians cannot be taken lightly during the World Cup. Sri Lanka would be aware of the North American team's confident performance against England in the warm-up match. England managed to eke out a narrow 16-run win over Canada thanks to a Stuart Broad five-for following an unexpected assault from little-known Rizwan Cheema. Chasing 243, the Pakistan-born Cheema scored a blistering 93 off 71 balls and was involved in good stands with Zubin Surkari and Khurram Chohan to bring Canada close to the target before Broad wrapped things up for the English. However, what would not help Canada's cause was that their top five batsmen managed just 17 runs between them in the same match.
