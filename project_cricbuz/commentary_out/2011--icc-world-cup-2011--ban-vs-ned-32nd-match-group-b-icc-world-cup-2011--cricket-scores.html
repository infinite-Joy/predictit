England for one, will be most interested spectators for this match, since a Bangladesh victory would see England slipping below them in the points table.Bangladesh: Their victory against England was among the greatest in their relatively short history, and given the manner in which it was achieved, also among the most historically significant. Bangladesh have displayed some up-and-down form in the tournament so far though, and they must be wary of inconsistency striking them in this match. Their top-order batting has a completely altered look when Tamim Iqbal fires, and it will be essential for him to do so again. Amongst the bowlers, Shafiul Islam has been great, but Bangladesh will be expected to prepare their traditional spin-friendly pitches and deploy their spinners effectively. Shakib al Hasan has done a commendable job in bringing his team together in the aftermath of the West Indies win, and he has led from the front on the field as well. He will now have to ensure that the team's feet stay grounded, for what is a must-win match in Bangladesh's quest for a quarter-final spot.Netherlands: They pulled the short straw when the schedule for the World Cup was announced, having to face England, South Africa, India and the West Indies as their first four teams. They have shown great spirit even though they have lost each of their matches - enough to be considered a genuine threat against the relatively less experienced Bangladesh and Ireland sides. Nothing exemplified that spirit better than Pieter Seelar's unbounded joy at getting Sachin Tendulkar out at Delhi. After his first match heroics, Ryan ten Doeschate has not lived up to his status as the best cricketer amongst Associate nations, and this is the best platform for him to do that. Netherlands will not be satisfied with merely making up the numbers in Group B, and will be fiercely targeting a victory here.Group Dynamics:Bangladesh have two matches in hand - the one against Netherlands and then against South Africa.
