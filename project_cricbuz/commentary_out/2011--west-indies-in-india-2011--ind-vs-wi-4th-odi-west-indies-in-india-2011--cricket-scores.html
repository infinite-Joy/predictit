Not many gave the visitors a chance of doing well in India, but they have fought hard and competed well, right from the start of the Test series, though they lost it 0-2. The ODI part of the tour started in a similar fashion; though the West Indies competed, they failed. However, the victory in the third ODI might well turn out to be the turning point of the tour and give the visitors some much needed lift in spirits. India: The hosts have made only one change to the squad that played the first 3 games, with Irfan Pathan replacing Umesh Yadav, who has to leave early for the Australian tour. The replacement should be a good boost to the batting line up more than the bowling attack, as it is India's batsmen who have uncharacteristically failed in home conditions. The batting woes for India start right at the top - Sehwag, Gambhir and Parthiv Patel are yet to put up a meaningful score in the tournament, which has made the batting line up heavily reliant on Rohit Sharma and Virat Kohli. The duo, likely to compete for the number 6 spot in Australia, have handled the responsibility well and have been sturdy in the middle order. Rohit Sharma, who is making a comeback from injury, in particular is having a dream series; he has scores of 72, 90 not out and 95 in the 3 matches. India will also hope for the lower middle order to come to the party - Suresh Raina has neither had luck nor runs come his way so far, but R Ashwin has shown ability with the bat.India's main concerns in the bowling line up will be the way the bowlers have succumbed to pressure in the death overs. India's pacers start well with the new ball but often allow the opposition to recover and punish them in the death. The strong batting line up often masks this concern, but West Indies made them pay in the 3rd ODI, where India allowed Darren Sammy and Andre Russell to put on an unbeaten 79 run partnership in just 34 balls. The pick of the bowlers so far have been Vinay Kumar and Umesh Yadav - the duo have consistently given the early breakthroughs and put West Indies in pressure early in the innings. India will be without the latter for the remaining 2 matches, and would hope that the replacement (likely to be Irfan Pathan) can continue the good work.West Indies: West Indies have twice managed to get out of jail thanks to an attacking lower order which has the likes of Russell, Sammy and Rampaul. The rescue acts by the tail may be exciting, but the hosts would know that they need more runs from their top order. The job may get tougher for them, as Darren Bravo - the only consistent West Indies batsman of the tour is not available; he was forced to retire hurt during his team's batting due to a hamstring injury and has been ruled out of the fourth ODI as well.
