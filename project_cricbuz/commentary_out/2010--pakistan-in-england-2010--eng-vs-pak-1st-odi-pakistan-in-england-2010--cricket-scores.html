Ajmal Shahzad has also been roped in for the ODIs, and the quickie will look cement his place with a worthy performance. The team has a nice balance with a good mixture of experience and youth in all the departments and competition for places is sure to keep all the players on their toes and give their best.FormEngland have won only 2 of their last five ODIs, but that will not matter much as they clearly hold the upper hand after victories in both the Test and T20 series. Their overall record in ODIs this year though, has been good with 9 victories from 12 matches. The caveat though is that 6 of the wins came against minnows like Bangladesh and Scotland. They will thus relish a chance to play and beat a top team.Pakistan have had a disastrous year so far, with their form deserting them and the spot-fixing controversy putting them in the news for the wrong reasons. Their form in the ODIs has been nothing short of pathetic. They have only managed to win once in the eight matches played this year and that too against lowly Bangladesh. They will be fervently hoping to better that record and start with a victory.Pakistan aren't new to controversy and no one knows better than them to bounce back after plumbing to the depths. They have often let their cricket do the talking with some stellar performances in the past when they have been surrounded by controversy or experiencing bad form, but they will be facing a squad whose confidence is sky high and form is superb. How they will get themselves out of the mess they find themselves in, will be interesting.
