"I watched that match on the telly. I was in school. Obviously, that was a starting point in Irish cricket. It was a great day," middle-order batsman, Andrew Balbirnie said. "It's just another game of the group in terms of preparation," skipper William Porterfield told reporters during the pre-match press conference. "Obviously result-wise it's a key match, but you are still going to prepare like you do for each match," he added.On the other hand, Ireland have been steady. After two wins - against West Indies and UAE - reality hit them during a massive defeat against South Africa. The game against Zimbabwe was a cliff-hanger and John Mooney's controversial catch turned out to be the difference between the two teams.
