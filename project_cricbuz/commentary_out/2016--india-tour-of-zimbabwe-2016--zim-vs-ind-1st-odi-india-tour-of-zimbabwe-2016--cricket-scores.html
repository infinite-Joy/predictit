With senior pros granted rest, Fazal could finally get to pull on the Indian jersey and slot into Shikhar Dhawan's opening position, alongside KL Rahul. In tough batting conditions, a lot will revolve around the middle-order core of Manish Pandey, Ambati Rayudu and Karun Nair, who will be raring to leave an indelible mark if he is handed his ODI debut on Saturday (June 11). MS Dhoni should be the rock in the middle, guiding his young team to play to expectations.With the likes of Elton Chigumbura and Chamu Chibhabha leading Zimbabwe's batting attack, the starts provided by Jasprit Bumrah and Jaydev Unadkat will go a long way in shaping the result as well as their futures in the 50-over format of the game. While Bumrah comes in after having established his credentials in T20Is, Unadkat faces the tough challenge of proving his mettle. The Saurashtra pacer, who returned from a long injury lay-off, enjoyed a good Ranji trophy season wherein he picked up 20 wickets in the first two fixtures.There could be a sense of friendly competitive spirit between Unadkat and Barinder Sran, who made his India debut in Australia last year and proved to be a vital member of the IPL 2016-winning Sunrisers Hyderabad squad. The series could also come as a stepping stone for Yuzvendra Chahal, the leg-spinner who has been one of Royal Challengers Bangalore's most prized assets for two seasons in a row. He will fight for the spinner's spot along with newbie Jayant Yadav and Axar Patel.Zimbabwe: The think-tank took severe measures to address the team's recent failure at the World T20. Dav Whatmore and Hamilton Masakadza, captain and coach of the side, paid the price for the ignominy of Zimbabwe being the only full member of ICC not qualifying for the Super 10s in the showpiece event in India. While bowling coach Makhaya Ntini takes over on a temporary basis, Greame Cremer has been named the interim skipper. Zimbabwe will miss the services of Tinashe Panyangara, the fast bowler, but, in Taurai Muzarabani, they have an able replacement.Sanjay Bangar, the Indian coach for the tour, acknowledged that his team need to adjust quickly to the conditions. The track in Harare is a tough one to bat on and the hosts will rely on their bowling attack to stifle a line-up that hasn't got too much international experience under its belt. Medium pacers Donald Tiripano and Neville Madziva will hold the aces early on. With Tendai Chisoro and Cremer assuming the duties of spinners, it remains to be seen if Wellington Masakadza, the young leg spinner who picked up four wickets against Scotland in the World T20 qualifier, gets a chance early in the series.Did you know? It will be his 197th ODI for Zimbabwe in addition to three for Africa XI.Harare Sports Club has hosted 126 ODIs, none being played under lights.
