PreviewEngland's poor run outside home continued, when they wasted a good start by two debutants to go down to the West Indies by 15 runs in the first of three ODIs in Antigua on Friday.Michael Lumb's debut hundred went in vain as an England line-up, without the assurance of Alastair Cook, Eoin Morgan and the sacked Kevin Pietersen, wilted under pressure and let the hosts run away with the game. There is not much experience in the England middle-order and it is therefore important for Lumb to bat like a seasoned pro, despite just one ODI and 20 T20 internationals under his belt.Lumb has been active in the English domestic scene for the last 14 years, and he will be an important asset for stand-in captain Stuart Broad, as will be the likes of Ravi Bopara, Jos Butler and Joe Root. Morgan, after missing the first game, appears to be unlikely to for the second game on Sunday as well, as England may not want to risk him ahead of the World T20.The bowling, again will have to revolve around the captain and he would expect the likes of Tim Bresnan and Chris Jordan to chip in with valuable spells.West Indies on the other hand, were boosted by skipper Dwayne Bravo and former skipper Darren Sammy, whose brutal hitting, coupled with Lendl Simmons' composed innings, helped the hosts to a sizeable score, even in the absence of the explosive Chris Gayle at the top.England are coming off a horror summer in Australia and they would not want to go down in another series, despite the lack of experience. Jason Holder, Ravi Rampaul, Dwayne Bravo and Sunil Narne make a formidable bowling unit but it will be important for England to hold their own and follow the examples of Lumb and Ali, who did not score a century but still contributed with a solid 59-ball 44.Stats- 1998: The last time West Indies won a bilateral ODI series against England at home.
