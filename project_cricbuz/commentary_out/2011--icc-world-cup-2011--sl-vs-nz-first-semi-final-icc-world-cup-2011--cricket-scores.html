Overall, Sri Lanka have so far played New Zealand 8 times in a World Cup match with the Island nation emerging victorious on 5 occasions while losing 3 times.Build up to the game:Sri Lanka: The co-hosts of the 2011 World Cup have lived up to their reputation as tournament favourites, making it quite comfortably to the semi-final stage. Barring a loss against Pakistan and the rain-affected match against Australia which was abandoned, Sri Lanka have routed the other sides they faced en-route to the final-four stage, including a thumping win against New Zealand in the group match. Their quarter-final match against England in Colombo might have been a close affair considering the home side were chasing under lights at the R Premadasa stadium, but Tillakaratne Dilshan and Upul Tharanga scored impressive hundreds to set-up a massive 10-wicket victory to send England crashing. Coming to the Lankan batting, the top four have been the key contributors for Sri Lanka during this tournament. Dilshan heads the chart as the Lankan top-scorer followed by skipper Kumar Sangakkara, Tharanga, and Mahela Jayawardene. Between them, these four have scored 6 hundreds so far in this World Cup, thus outlining the fact that they are the batting pillars in the team. The weakness lies in the next three, who between them have only scored 210 runs so far. Mathews, Samaraweera and Chamara Silva are yet to shine in the World Cup, and if there is a top-order collapse then these three will be more than tested. The Lankan bowling has been a disciplined outfit and the likes of Murali, Malinga, Kulasekara and Mendis have not let the opposition batsmen get off the hook. Murali is the highest wicket taker so far for Sri Lanka with 13 scalps followed by Malinga with 8. Dilshan has also had a instrumental role to play in the bowling department, containing the batsmen as well as picking up crucial wickets. However, Sri Lanka would be concerned with the fitness of Muralitharan who has a 50-50 chance of playing this game after aggravated a quadricep muscle during the match against England. With a boisterous crowd backing them and also with key players in good form, Sri Lanka will be slight favourites in this game but should be wary of the New Zealanders ability to upset the big teams.New Zealand: The Kiwis entered the World Cup after a tumultuous 2010 during which they were blanked 0-4 in the ODI series against Bangladesh, suffered a overwhelming 0-5 loss against India and also were at the end of a 2-3 series loss against Pakistan at home. The Black-Caps were perceived as a team which could be defeated easily and at the maximum were expected only to reach the final eight. However, the Kiwis have shown that they are spirited fighters and the inclusion of John Wright and Allan Donald in the coaching staff has worked wonders as the New Zealanders ensured a semi-final date by pulling off what might be termed as an upset, to choke South Africa out of the tournament. When compared to the Sri Lankan top-four, New Zealand's main batsmen have been only average in the tournament, barring Ross Taylor, who has been the Kiwi main-stay. Taylor, in the 5 innings that he has played so far, has scored 288 runs at an average of 72 including the fabulous century he scored against Pakistan in the group stage. But the runs have not flown that frequently from the bats of Brendon McCullum, Martin Guptill and Jesse Ryder, although it must be said that Ryder's composed knock of 83 against South Africa proved crucial for New Zealand's entry into the semi-finals. A combined effort from the top four along with contributions from the likes of Styris, Oram and Williamson is a must, considering the spin-heavy Sri Lankan attack which the Kiwis are yet to master. When it comes to bowling, Tim Southee has been the stand-out performer for the Black-Caps, bagging 15 wickets at an average of 17. He has been well supported by Jacob Oram (12 wickets) and Nathan McCullum (8 wickets) even as injuries have plagued the Kiwi attack throughout the World Cup. Hamish Bennett and Kyle Mills are out of the World Cup and currently in the squad as their replacements are Daryl Tuffey and Andy McKay. Although he may not make much of an impact against the Lankan batters, who are known to play spin well, he would certainly curb down the flow of runs and keep one end tight.
