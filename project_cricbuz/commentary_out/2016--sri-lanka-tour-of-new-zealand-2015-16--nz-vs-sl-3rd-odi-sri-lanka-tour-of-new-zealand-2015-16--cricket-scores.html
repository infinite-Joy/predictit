The two teams couldn't have had more contrasting years. While New Zealand made giant strides across formats to establish themselves as a genuine powerhouse of the sport, Sri Lanka grappled with the loss of batting stalwarts and have been in a perpetual state of transition. This particular tour of New Zealand has already seen everything from failed drug tests to a beeline of players heading to the treatment table, with lead pacer Lasith Malinga becoming the latest to be ruled out of the tour. Angelo Mathews began the year with a Test and ODI series defeat in New Zealand and runs the risk of ending the year with an encore at the Saxton Oval unless he can inspire his beleaguered side to turn the series around. The manner of their 'humiliating 'defeat in Christchurch - New Zealand chased down 117 in under nine overs - would suggest that salvaging anything from the series appears far from probable. However, when faced with a similar predicament in the Test series, Sri Lanka put on a spirited effort in Hamilton and with a little application, stood a chance to level the series. The popular belief is that in a series where so much has gone wrong, things can only get better. However, sport is no place for popular beliefs. Negative spirals can be difficult to get out of and the experienced heads in the side - Tillakaratne Dilshan, Mathews and Nuwan Kulasekara - will now be tasked to lift a bunch of drooping heads in the side. Notwithstanding their almost non-existent batting, Sri Lanka's bowling has left much to be desired and young Dushmantha Chameera's fixation with the short-pitch deliveries after his success in the Hamilton Test hasn't boded well in the limited overs. Kulasekara and his fellow medium pacers will have to exercise some control at the top of the order for Sachithra Senanayake and Jeffrey Vandersay to have any impact in the middle overs. New Zealand, who have not lost a game all series, will fancy their chances to put the ODI series to bed in Nelson. The last time the two sides played each other at this venue, a Kane Williamson century eclipsed the Sangakkara-Jayawardene show as the hosts chased down 276 in a canter. The 25-year-old Williamson is expected to return from injury to lead the side with Brendon McCullum sidelined due to a back injury. Tim Southee and Adam Milne have been passed fit to play but Trent Boult has been penciled in for a return only in the fifth ODI.
