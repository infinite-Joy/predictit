Alastair Cook and Craig Kieswetter have formed an useful alliance at the top of the order while Jonathan Trott continues to be a run-machine. The outright positivity of Kevin Pietersen and the adventurous Eoin Morgan give the middle order not just stability but also the big hitting ability. For a batsman of his class, Ian Bell seems to a waste at 6 though . England might have to rethink about their batting order in the event of a massive chase like the one yesterday at Headingly. Cook and Trott are solid, but tend to take time to settle putting the lower middle order batsmen under pressure. A swap in the batting order between Pietersen and Trott will lend more balance and power to this unit.The fact that Tim Bresnan, Stuart Broad and Graeme Swann are no mugs with the bat afford England to play the extra bowler.
