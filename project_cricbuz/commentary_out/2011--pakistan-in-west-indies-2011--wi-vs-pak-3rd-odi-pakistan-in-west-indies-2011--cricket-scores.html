Their performances have been clinical in both the games, with both departments ensuring that they were never outplayed. West Indies on the other hand have a lot to do to bounce back into the series.West IndiesThe West Indies have not had many bright spots in the series so far. Lendl Simmons has been their stand out batsman, with a couple of steady shows but the others have failed to come good.Marlon Samuels' return to the West Indies main team has been close to disastrous. He struggled during his last outing - managing to score 29 runs off 74 balls. At one stage, the once feared batsman was 3 off 36. Darren Bravo, Simmons and Dwayne Bravo hold the key for the calypso kings while batting and the trio have to come good if West Indies are to prevent going into last 2 games thinking about damage control.Their bowlers haven't been helped in both games by their batters putting up less than competitive scores. Devendra Bishoo has been the only bowler in their line up to have anything in the wickets column, getting 2 wickets each in the 2 games. The other bowlers have failed to cause any kind of positive impact against a disciplined Pakistan batting line up.PakistanPakistan have few lesser problems when compared to the West Indies, the biggest of the lot being their middle order not being fully tested yet.Their top order has come good in the series, and in fine style. Their top 4 look in good touch and Ahmed Shehzad's 100 in the last game will have given him truckloads of confidence. Their batters will have a pretty clear game plan for the 3rd ODI - do exactly the same as the first 2 ODIs. Their middle order though is yet to be seriously tested and it will be interesting to see how they fare should the situation arise.Their bowlers have been successful in restricting the West Indies to less than power scores on both occasions and they look good for a repeat. Saeed Ajmal has proven to be impossible to play against for WI and the off spinner will take that confidence with him to get more wickets for himself.Players to watch out forDwayne Bravo: The West Indian all rounder has a massive job on his hands trying to inspire his team to bounce back. With loads of experience and talent, Bravo is WI's best bet.Saeed Ajmal: The Pakistani off spinner has spun a web around the West Indians with his guile. Another repeat should help Pakistan seal the series.
