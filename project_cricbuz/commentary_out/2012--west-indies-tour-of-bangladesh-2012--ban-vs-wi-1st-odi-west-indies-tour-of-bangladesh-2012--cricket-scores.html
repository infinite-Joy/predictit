With some big hitters coming in to join the One Day squad, the visitors might just be able to come up trumps in the limited overs series.Team NewsBangladesh: The hosts will be missing a key player in Shakib Al Hasan, someone who hasn't missed an ODI since 2008. The absence of a Shakib will surely leave a hug void in Bangladesh's line up, one they might struggle to fill. Mominul Haque has been called in to replace the all rounder but there is sure to be some pressure on the uncapped 21 year old.The batting will depend heavily on the likes of Tamim Iqbal and Mushfiqur Rahim. Iqbal has been a regular feature in Bangladesh's limited overs outings and has the experience and the ability to put together a big innings at a good pace. Bangladesh has the benefit of being able to field a few all rounders, in Mahmudullah and Naeem Islam.As far as the bowling is concerned, the pace attack should be spearheaded by Mashrafe Mortaza and the experienced campaigner should be backed up by Rubel Hossain. As usual, Bangladesh will look to employ a lot of spin by playing Abur Razzak and could include Sohag Gazi, someone who made quite an impact in the Test series. The side looks like a balanced one, however, the absence of Shakib is bound to deplete the team of some quality.West Indies: Darren Sammy and his side are coming of a fine period of cricket and the morale in the dressing room is bound to be on a high. Chris Gayle wasn't at his fiery best during the Test series, however, the left handed opener should feel a lot more comfortable in the limited overs format. Dwayne Smith could be asked to open alongside Gayle and in that case, West Indies would have an opening pair that could devastate just about any attack. The likes of Lendl Simmons and Darren Bravo add a lot of stability to this batting line up, while the lower middle order comprises of some all rounders who can really give the ball a whack.Marlon Samuels, Kieron Pollard, Andre Russell and Sammy add a lot of balance to this side by contributing immensely with the bat and ball. The bowling looks quite the part as well, with Kemar Roach and Ravi Rampaul leading the pace attack. One man who will be under the spotlight is Sunil Narine. The mystery spinner had a rather mysterious time in the Test series as he failed to make a bug impact and Narine will be required to contribute a lot more in the limited overs format.
