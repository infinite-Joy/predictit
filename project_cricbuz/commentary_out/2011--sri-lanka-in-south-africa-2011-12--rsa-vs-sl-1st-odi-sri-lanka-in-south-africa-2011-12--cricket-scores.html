Despite the cynicism surrounding them, the visitors did manage to end their losing streak in the longer format with a memorable victory at Durban. South Africa bounced back to win the next match (and hence the series) at Cape Town, but the visitors will be silently confidentabout their chances in the ODI series, especially after their Durban win. The ODI series also poses contrasting challenges for the rival skippers; Sri Lanka's Dilshan has had a rough time ever since he took charge, while South Africa's AB de Villiers will look for a good start in his new role. Sri Lanka: Sri Lanka's ODI performances suffered a steep slide ever along with their Test performances, ever since they ended up as runners up in the World Cup in April, 2011. Sri Lanka, looking for a fresh start under new skipper Tillakaratne Dilshan, began by surrendering to the Australians in home conditions. If losing to Australia at home wasn't enough, the Lankans suffered an embarrassing 1-4 defeat at the hands of Pakistan in the UAE. If anything, the series in South Africa will pose a tougher challenge, but Sri Lanka will take some positives out of the recently concluded Test series. The batting line up wears a familiar look and is heavily dependent on the seniors - Sangakkara, Dilshan and Jayawardene. Lasith Malinga's presence in the ODI squad should bolster the pace attack, as he will enjoy the pacer friendly conditions in South Africa. The venue for the first ODI - Paarl - is known to be one of South Africa's slowest tracks, which could suit the visitors. The nature of the wicket could also make Sri Lanka play Ajantha Mendis, who did not feature in the Tests, along with left arm spinner Rangana Herath. Mendis and Herath were impressive in the tour game against Emerging Cape Cobras, picking up 3 wickets each to spin Sri Lanka to a victory. South Africa: South Africa's home ODI series against Sri Lanka will mark the birth of a new chapter in their limited overs cricket. Like their opponents, South Africa too seeked a change of guard post the World Cup and appointed a new skipper in AB de Villiers for the One Day Internationals. The Proteas did play a short series against the visiting Australians, but a hand injury kept de Villiers out of the squad, leaving the captaincy job to Hashim Amla. The task for the new captain became tougher on the day the squad was announced, as he was also selected as the wicket-keeper in place of out-of-form Mark Boucher. De Villiers has the luxury of a settled squad to assist him in his new innings, and even has former skipper Graeme Smith and senior players like Kallis to turn to. Contrary to the Sri Lankans, South Africa's ODI squad wears a different look compared to the team that played the Tests. Only six of the South Africans played the Tests, compared to the 10 in the Sri Lankan squad. Even Vernon Philander - the new sensation in the SA Test squad - failed to get a place in the limited overs version. Leg spinner Imran Tahir misses out as well, and ODI regulars Johan Botha and Robin Peterson are the two spinners in the squad. The injury to batsman Dean Elgar provides another chance to all rounder Albie Morkel, who hasn't featured in the national squad since September 2010. Pacer Rory Kleinveldt, who has appeared in Twenty20 internationals, is the only player in the squad who does not have an ODI cap, and might have to wait for his turn as South Africa already have enough pacers in Dale Steyn, Morne Morkel and Wayne Parnell.
