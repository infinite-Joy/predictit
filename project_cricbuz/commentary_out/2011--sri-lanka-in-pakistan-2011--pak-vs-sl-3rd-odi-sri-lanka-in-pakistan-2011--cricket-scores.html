Both Pakistan and Sri Lanka have won a game each in the 5 match series and now is the time either team will look to gain the advantage. Usually the momentum, and in most cases the winner, is decided by the momentum shift that occurs midway through a series. The teams are as closely matched in talent and preparation as the scoreline 1-1 would suggest. Pakistan, having won the Test series convincingly romped home in the first game. The Lankan batsmen, who failed to pass muster in that game, looked set for another collapse in the second ODI as well, but managed to keep their nerves. Led by their bowlers they inflicted a damage similar to the one on their batting order, on the opponents. Scores settled, both teams will look to inflict new wounds. Pakistan: By far the most impressive and most improved team amongst their contemporaries, they were given a severe jolt by the loss in the second ODI. They looked a compact side till then with a colourful variety in their bowling attack. Bolstered by the return of Afridi and Razzaq, they have been spoilt for choice and the fact that the dangerous Junaid Khan is sitting on the bench goes to show the quality of their eleven. While their ability to pick up 10 wickets cannot be questioned, there are a few chinks in the batting armour. Firstly, they don't have a free flowing and quick scoring batter in their top order. Though Afridi and Razzaq have in the past performed that role with much success, the Pakistan management seems hesitant to use them in that role. This handicap means that if wickets are lost in succession, the scoring rate also plummets. Although Misbah and Younis provide strength on the basis of their experience, their style of batting is not well suited to the current T20 attitude. Umar Akmal, who dazzled in the previous match with a knock of 91 that almost saw his team through, can be promoted up the order. The team will also look to Afridi and Razzaq for some fireworks in the powerplay for the 2nd ODI was lost in the batting powerplay where they could manage only 26/3 as compared to Sri Lanka's 48/0. The bowling has been much talked about, but they might want to key up their catching in the outfield which will make their bowlers look even more dangerous.Sri Lanka: The Lankan side has looked out of sorts since Murali's retirement. Their bowling performance in the Tests has been abject, but given the fact that they are the World Cup finalists, a stronger showing was expected in the ODIs. The Sri Lankan opening pair have been the ones to give a strong start since the 1996 World cup and nothing less is expected from Dilshan and Tharanga. Although the latter responded well sculpting a slow 77, the skipper needs to fire because when he does, his team will be propelled to a big score. The middle order looks weak with Sangakkara and Chandimal not in the best touch, but Jayawardene's return to form will boost their confidence. Malinga and Perera throttled Pakistan's batsmen with accurate bowling and though they will look to repeat the show, the ineffectiveness of the spinners will be a worry. Suraj Randiv might be drafted into the playing eleven as he is an excellent fielder inside the 30 yard circle apart from being a good off spinner.
