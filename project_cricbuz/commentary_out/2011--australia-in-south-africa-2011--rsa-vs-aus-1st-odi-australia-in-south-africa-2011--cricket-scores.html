We shall know in a short whileHello and welcome to the first ODI between South Africa and Australia at Centurion, South Africa. Both the teams are evenly matched, proof of which was given by the 1-1 standing of the 2 match T20 series, and whoever wins this first match will surely gain an upper hand psychologically. We will bring you the toss in a short while.Preview by Saurabh SomaniSouth Africa and Australia have always provided sharp-edged contests over the years, and their recently concluded Twenty20 series was a fine example of that, with the spoils being shared 1-1. The ODI series that is about to get underway is not likely to be any different. Both teams have traditionally always been amongst the stronger ones in world cricket, and both have the distinction of playing two of the most memorable ODI matches of all time - the tied semi-final in the 1999 World Cup and the match that's called "the 434 game". While the current ODI series may not produce such matches, it is more or less certain that it won't produce dull cricket.South Africa: The hosts wear a considerably altered look from the side that took to the field for the Twenty20 matches. Returning to the squad are the rested Jacques Kallis and Dale Steyn - each man well capable of winning a match on his own. Where South Africa might be lacking is in the top-order batting. Without the giant presence of AB de Villiers, it wears a thinner than normal look, especially given the poor form that Graeme Smith has been experiencing. The burden of scoring is going to rest on Hashim Amla, Kallis and JP Duminy in all likelihood. The bowling appears considerably stronger. Dale Steyn and Morne Morkel will lead the attack and South Africa have the luxury of choosing either Lonwabo Tsotsobe or Wayne Parnell as the third seamer. There are a host of spinners with Imran Tahir, Johan Botha and Robin Peterson - and Botha's batting ability could swing the selection his way. If the hosts want to gamble, they can include the impressive Tahir too and see how Australia handle quality spin.Australia: One of Australia's biggest worries ahead of the match is the fitness of Shane Watson. With his top-order batting and seam-up bowling, Watson's presence lends an incredible balance to the team, but he had to sit out of the second T20 with a niggle and it's not yet certain that he will recover for the first ODI. The man who replaced him - Mitchell Marsh - did a very good job though, and Watson's absence will be another opportunity for the junior Marsh to strut his stuff. Like South Africa, Australia too sport a new look from the T20s, with seniors Michael Clarke and Ricky Ponting being the most prominent faces returning. Again like the hosts, the visitors are also missing a key player in Brett Lee. In Lee's absence, the bowling will be led by Mitchell Johnson and Doug Bollinger and it remains to be seen if the young and undeniably quick Pat Cummins is gambled on. Australia have Xavier Doherty and Steve Smith to choose from as their spinning option.Captain vs Captain:Hashim Amla: His current role may be that of a stand-in captain, but it is as a batsman that Amla has really shone. Thought to be not fit for ODIs once upon a time, this will be his 50th ODI match. His statistics in the format are jaw-dropping: an average of over 57 and a strike-rate of almost 93. With a great deal of the batting burden on him, the captaincy, and a mini-landmark of the 50th ODI to boot, the spotlight is squarely on the soft-spoken South African.Michael Clarke: Since assuming the Australian captaincy full-time, Michael Clarke has scored his runs at an average of 97.50. The catch, of course, is that it's been only 8 matches and 7 innings, but it does point to his recent good form. Captaincy doesn't appear to have weighed him down, though the current series will be a tougher test than any other he has faced so far. However, Clarke has seemed to enjoy the captaincy so far, and the responsibility has made him more hungry for runs.
