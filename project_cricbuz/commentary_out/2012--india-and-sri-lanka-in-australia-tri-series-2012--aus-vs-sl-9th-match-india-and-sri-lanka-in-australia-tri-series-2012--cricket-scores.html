A win for either team will place them closer towards a berth in the finals while a loss for Sri Lanka will enliven India's hopes.Australia: The hosts seemed to be surging ahead of its competitors before two successive losses held them back a bit. They found the winning formula again to beat India at the Gabba and head the table. The top-order batting has remained an area of concern for the hosts. David Warner has surprisingly struggled to adapt to the ODI form of the game. For an effective hitter of the ball, he has been found out whenever the bowlers change their angles and cramp him for room. Warner did show some of his ability against India at the Gabba and Australia will hope for more from their premium opener. The middle order has acquitted themselves very well with the Hussey brothers and Peter Forrest in charge while Daniel Christian has emerged as the finisher.Australia have an array of richness in the bowling department and the return of Ryan Harris strengthens the attack further. Ben Hilfenhaus continued to harass the Indians in the shorter format and marked his return to the ODI team after a span of more than 2 years with a 5-wicket haul. Clint McKay leads the bowling department with 9 scalps but may find it difficult to break into the already powerful bowling unit. Xavier Doherty has impressed all with his left arm spin, managing to keep the batsmen of the two Asian powerhouses quiet.Australia will be hoping that both their batting and bowling units click as they look to strengthen their position on the points table.Sri Lanka: After a slow start, Sri Lanka have hit top gear in superlative fashion. They crushed the hosts at Sydney with a bonus point before thrashing their sub-continental neighbours India at the Gabba. They have finally got the right batting order resulting in most of their successes. Mahela Jayawardene has been a revelation as a captain and his batting showed vast improvements at the top of the order. Dinesh Chandimal has been consistent in the middle order while Lahiru Thirimanne batted beautifully at the Gabba. Angelo Mathews has been a picture of consistency as a finisher while the likes of Thissara Perera and Fervez Maharoof have added stability and depth to the batting.Coming to Sri Lanka's bowling, Lasith Malinga has continued with his wicket taking form although being a touch expensive at times. Nuwan Kulasekara overcame a sluggish start to emerge as the side's best bowler in the last couple of matches. The all-rounders too have chipped in with useful contributions with the ball, making run-scoring difficult for the opposition. Rangana Herath has been very tidy with his left arm orthodox to round off a very good and balanced bowling attack.Mahela's inspired captaincy has seen Sri Lanka emerge stronger after a poor start and they will seek to continue their winning momentum.Team News: Shane Watson has made a comeback into the ODI squad after recovering from a hamstring injury sustained during the tour of South Africa. The chief of the National Selection Panel, John Inverarity has however hinted that Watson might not be risked for Friday's clash. In the absence of Ponting, Clarke is slated to bat at No.3 with Daniel Christian set to retain his place. Ryan Harris will be back at the expense of Mitchell Starc and Hilfenhaus's form will keep out Clint McKay.Sri Lanka have hit a purple patch in the last two matches and may go with an unchanged line-up once again.Quotes:It is tough not having the great Ricky Ponting out there this game playing one-day cricket for us but that's the decision we've made - Michael Clarke accepts that he had a role in Ricky Ponting's axing from the ODI squad.I have (spoken to him).
