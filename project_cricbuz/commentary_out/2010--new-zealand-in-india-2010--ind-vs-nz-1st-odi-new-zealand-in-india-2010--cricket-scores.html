The fact that was also obscured by the screaming 4-0 headlines versus Bangladesh was that three of those ODIs were very narrow defeats for New Zealand - twice by 9 runs and once by 3 runs. It is not, therefore, that they have suddenly become a very poor ODI side from a competitive one. It is, however, undeniable that they have been sub-par, and that the pressure on them to raise their game is more acute.It is also New Zealand's last series in the subcontinent before the World Cup, but they are unlikely to tinker with too much of the core of Vettori, Taylor, Mills, the McCullums and Williamson.
