The last time they won all the games in a five-match series was in 2006-07 when a Habibul Bashar-led Bangladesh beat a visiting Zimbabwe side. They got close against New Zealand in 2010-11 by winning the series 4-0, but one of the matches was abandoned.Bangladesh:In this series, Bangladesh have been good at wiggling out off potentially tricky situations. In the last game, they recovered from 32 for 4 to post 256 for 8, before their bowlers managed to suffocate the Zimbabwe chase. In fact, Bangladesh have had different people taking up responsibilities at various stages of the series.Tamin Iqbal, Anamul Haque, Shakib Al Hasan, Mushfiqur Rahim and Mahmudullah have all posted 50+ scores in the series. The return to form of Mahmudullah in the last game particularly pleased the team management. Imrul Kayes, if given another chance in the place of Mominul Haque, after his failure in the fourth ODI, would look to make a case for his inclusion in the World Cup squad. Soumya Sarkar, a fast bowling all-rounder, has been added to the squad and might get a chance to impress the selectors, as Bangladesh look to avoid complacency and achieve a confidence-boosting whitewash.Zimbabwe:Zimbabwe have put up better performances as the series has progressed, but unfortunately, not been able to cross the final mile. Solomon Mire's performances with both the bat and the ball have given them a good lift.
