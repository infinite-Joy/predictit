Almost a year later, we are back to Eden Park for the beginning of the Chappell-Hadlee Trophy. Hello and a warm welcome to the first ODI of the 3-match series. All eyes would be on Brendon McCullum, who has announced that he will retire after the Test series later this month. Both the hosts and the visitors are fresh from series wins (in the ODIs) over Pakistan and India respectively.Preview:For New Zealand, it be will be the beginning of an end when they take on trans-Tasman rivals Australia in the first One-Day International (ODI) of the Chappell-Hadlee Trophy at Eden Park in Auckland on Wednesday (February 3).Brendon McCullum, who is set to retire after the T20I series later in the month, will look to bid adieu to his ODI career on a note as pleasant as the entertainment he has provided to the cricket world so far.McCullum will be leading a fairly inexperienced side that will be without the services of Ross Taylor, Tim Southee and Mitchell McClenaghan. Nonetheless, with the likes of Trent Boult, Doug Bracewell, Matt Henry and Adam Milne in the squad, they possess as strong a bowling attack as any side in the world.The world champions Australia will be looking to continue what they did against India ( a series that they won 4-1)even as injury woes continue to keep quite a few players out of action. The hamstring strain to Aaron Finch will allow the team management to try out more options from the vast reservoir of in-form batsmen they boast of.Shaun Marsh is set to start in the first ODI, a move that unfortunately results in the snub of Usman Khawaja. "He does need a hit going into a big Test series for us and he's batting extremely well. He's unlucky to miss out but Shaun's getting the first opportunity. He played well in Australia and he's batting extremely well as well," Steve Smith said ahead of the first ODI.Josh Hazlewood, who has been out of action for a while, is set to lead the attack on Wednesday. Young leg-spinner Adam Zampa and pacer Scott Boland too will sit out in the first clash of the series."We've got quite an inexperienced bowling attack. The last time the two sides played an ODI in New Zealand, it was at the Eden Park during the Group A encounter of the ICC World Cup last year.
