Dot balls were aplenty while there was a drought of boundaries when the West Indians were wielding the bat. Ramnaresh Sarwan and Marlon Samuels did score half-centuries, but the amount of time they took to get to that mark and their dismissals soon after reaching there, did not do much in boosting the Windies total. The Indians too had a laborious outing with the bat, needing to work hard to get to the target, which they eventually did thanks to fifties from Rohit Sharma and Shikhar Dhawan. Where the appreciation is due to the touring team is that the batsmen showed slightly better application in trying to adjust to the nature of the pitch and paced the innings intelligently to help their side emerge victors. Slow pitches, scores less than 250 and batsmen struggling before getting to the target was the feature of the WI-Pak ODI series. Nothing different can be expected for the second game as it is very unlikely that these sides would encounter even a slight bit of green on the 22-yard track.Missing the big-hitters:Whatever be the nature of the pitch, when players like Virender Sehwag and Chris Gayle are out there in the middle, the track makes little difference.
