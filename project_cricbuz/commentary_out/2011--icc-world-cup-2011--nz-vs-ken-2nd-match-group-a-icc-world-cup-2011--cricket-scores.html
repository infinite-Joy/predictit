Stay tuned for all the live updates, toss and team news.by Vishaal Loganathan:The format of the 2011 World Cup means the league phase will be thickly populated by established teams taking on minnows, and the second match of the event is one such. However, the established team involved - New Zealand - have had a rough time of it in international cricket in the past few months, while the minnows - Kenya - have a history of causing upsets in the World Cup.Team News:New Zealand: New Zealand have had more than their share of a taste of the sub continent in the past few months: they played Bangladesh in a 5 match series, then came to India for 5 more before going to back to play 7 ODIs against Pakistan. The series should have helped the Kiwis get a fair view of the conditions and the kind of players likely to come up against in the World Cup but the series against the two teams turned out to be a harsh reality check. The horrible slump in form put NZ in need of some serious soul searching ahead of the World Cup. The Kiwis will look to erase these painful memories and get their act together when they take on lowly Kenya in their opening match of the World Cup. To say that their batting in recent times has been lacklustre would be a huge understatement. The New Zealand batting, especially from the middle order, has been pathetic. Martin Guptill, at the top of the order, has been one of the very few batsmen to be amongst the runs and they have often had the lower order to thank for their gritty performances.Kenya: Kenya have hardly had anything to make the world stand up and take notice since their surprise rise to the semi-finals in the 2003 edition and that will be something they will take inspiration from. Another foray into the semis looks highly unlikely given that this year's tournament format favours the top teams. However, that will not stop them from trying to upset a few apple-carts along the way, as they did so memorably against the West Indies in 1996, when the World Cup was last held in the subcontinent. Kenya's main weapon would be the element of surprise. The Black Caps would have hardly seen, let alone play against, the Kenyan side. They can also take heart from the fact that New Zealand's performances of late have been bad, and this would be the ideal chance for them to start off with a bang.Captain's speak:New Zealand: Daniel Vettori said the team was looking at the World Cup to get a fresh start.
