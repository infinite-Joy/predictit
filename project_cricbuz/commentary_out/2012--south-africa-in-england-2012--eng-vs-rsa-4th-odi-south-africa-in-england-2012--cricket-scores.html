The South Africans, meanwhile, will be looking at the loss as an one-off affair and will be eager to get back to their winning ways.What's happened so far:After clinching the Test series 2-0, South Africa extended their winning run in the ODIs with a convincing 80 run win in the second match at Southampton. The first ODI at Cardiff was a wash-out, with only 5.3 overs being bowled during which England managed 37/0. The hosts finally managed to secure a victory when they beat South Africa at The Oval. Asked to bowl first, England managed to bowl out South Africa for 211 with Jade Dernbach (3/44) doing the damage early on and James Anderson (4/44) cleaning up the tail. In the chase, England lost wickets early on and were also in some trouble when the middle order stuttered a bit. But the knocks of Eoin Morgan (73) and Jonathan Trott (71) saw the team through.The hosts: England have been over-powered by South Africa for most part of the tour but they managed to have their way in the third ODI to turn the tables on South Africa. The bowlers, especially Dernbach and Anderson, came up with a good effort to skittle out South Africa for a sub-par total in the 3rd ODI. It was this kind of bowling effort that was missed by the English in the earlier matches, and the hosts will be hoping that the bowlers continue their good work for the rest of the series. Ravi Bopara's inclusion has also helped the team as he gives the hosts an extra option in the bowling department. With the likes of Steven Finn, James Tredwell and Samit Patel, England have a relatively settled bowling unit. As far as England's batting is concerned, the form of the top-order has been a concern for some time. Alastair Cook has had a couple of bad games while Ian Bell has also not been at his best in the opening slot. The skipper will be hoping that he and his opening partner get back to their scoring ways and give the team a good platform. Trott and Morgan failed in the second ODI but they came back well to contribute for their team in a winning cause and will be looking to continue the same. While Bopara's bowling has been an added advantage to his side, his batting form remains a concern and England will be hoping that he finds form soon. The hosts will have gained some confidence after their last win and if they put in a combined effort like they did at The Oval, they definitely can take the lead in the series.The visitors: Graeme Smith, Hashim Amla, AB de Villiers and JP Duminy - with this batting order, South Africa have in them a line-up that is capable of winning the match from any position.
