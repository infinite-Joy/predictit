With Samuels looking in red hot form and Imran Tahir still fresh from having steamrolled the West Indies a few nights ago, we are in for a crackerjack of a contest.With only one spot up for grabs in the final of the tri-series, West Indies and South Africa will take on each other in a do-or-die contest at Kensington Oval in Bridgetown on Friday (June 24). With two bonus points and a washed out match, South Africa are four points ahead of the hosts. However, the Jason Holder-led side will need only a win in the last match of the league phase to tie with South Africa on points, and with number of wins being the first criteria in case teams are tied on points, the hosts would seal a spot in the final.Unlike the initial stages of the tournament, the Kensington Oval track has played slightly on the quicker side. While there is more assistance for the pacers and the batsmen, spinners won't be as potent. Thus the most successful bowlers for West Indies and South Africa, so far in the series, Sunil Narine and Imran Tahir can't be relied upon too heavily.On the batting front, South Africa's top order batsmen Hashim Amla, Quinton de Kock and Faf du Plessis have had a good series. However, AB de Villiers and JP Duminy haven't come as handy. There was an odd fight by Farhaan Behardien and Wayne Parnell, but consistency still remains their nemesis.On the other hand, West Indies' story has been quite contrasting so far with the top order failing to fire. Their number four - Marlon Samuels - has had to do the bulk of the scoring, with Kieron Pollard's occasional blitz late down the order. Denesh Ramdin came good in the previous encounter, which augurs well for the team. On the consistency front, they remain a few notches below the Proteas.When: West Indies vs South Africa, June 23, 1:00 PM local (10:30 PM IST)Where: Kensington Oval, Bridgetown, BarbadosWhat to expect: It is expected to remain sunny for most parts of the day, with a couple of light spells of rains expected.Team News:Jason Holder remains a doubtful started for the crucial clash as he is still suffering from a hamstring injury. The decision on whether he will play or miss out will be taken on Friday afternoon, a few hours before the match. In case he fails to recover in time, Ramdin will lead the side.
