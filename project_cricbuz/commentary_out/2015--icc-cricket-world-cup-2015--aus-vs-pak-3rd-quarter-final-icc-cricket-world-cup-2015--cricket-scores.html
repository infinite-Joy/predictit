"I rate the moments after the World Cup victory as the most pleasing and satisfying of my career as I had never seen the people of Pakistan so happy," the International Cricket Council quoted him as saying. Imran's win had a philanthropic side attached to it as well as he used the funds generated from the win to realise his lifelong ambition of building a cancer hospital in memory of his late mother. "It sunk in that we won the World Cup only two or three days later. First of all, we couldn't sleep that night. I remember I had to share half a sleeping pill with Ijaz (Ahmed) to go to bed because we couldn't sleep till three or four in the morning."Imran Khan was one man who believed that his side could win even when his team-mates thought otherwise. Imran would say, at every team meeting: "We will win before we leave." The 12th man on that day at the MCG, Zahid Fazal, recalled how Imran kept believing despite an embarrassing performance against England in the league game. "After we were bowled out for 74, we packed our bags [because we thought we were finished]. But Imran insisted we would still win the World Cup."Imran had done it. Pakistan had done it. It was an epochal night for Pakistan, in front of a then-record crowd of 87,182 at the MCG. Imran had inspired his side to World Cup glory. At the age of 39. On his last legs. At the last attempt. After coming back from retirement. No-one could've written a script like that too. It was destined to end this way. Not in despair like in 1987. Sport did give him a fairytale ending. Destiny played its part too. The stars aligned themselves perfectly to make this possible. A champion had ended his career a World Cup winner. The World Cup sat comfortably in his arms. The circle was complete. The dream realised. The journey done. Imran Khan - World Cup winner. Pakistan - World Champions. Imran wanted to see this. He did.Ian Botham who was dismissed by Wasim Akram in the final felt that the left-arm seamer was the real deal. "The one player who really stood out for me was Wasim Akram. It was in that tournament that we realised just what a special talent he was and how much trouble he was going to give us and the rest of the world in the years to come. What a player he was."Here is another example of Imran's man-management skills. Wasim Akram had a huge problem with no-balls and wides during the 1992 World Cup. He was bowling with the white Kookaburra and found himself unable to control his swing. Also he repeatedly overstepped striving for more pace. Imran though trusted his ace and said this to the local newspapers: "I don't mind Wasim bowling no-balls as long as he bowls quick". Wasim repaid his captain's trust with a Man of the Match performance.So what did Imran have in mind for the final? Any new surprise? Yes there was one. He sported a t-shirt with a tiger's picture on it at the toss. Many were stunned and curious to know why he had done so. "If the boys play like tigers then I don't mind if they win or lose," he famously remarked. Before the final, he had asked his team to play like "Cornered Tigers". They did just that beating England by 22 runs with Imran fittingly taking the final wicket. Imran had promoted himself to three to protect the middle-order and his move proved to be a masterstroke. Along with Javed Miandad, he added 139 runs for the third wicket in 31 overs. Imran made 72 - which took Pakistan to 249. It was a huge decision to take, but Imran trusted himself so much. When he was not in form! Another of Imran's proteges, Wasim Akram lit up the MCG with a stunning display with ball in hand. Wasim had earlier made a cameo 18-ball 33 with the bat and was confidence personified with the ball.The semi-final win was one more example of Imran backing his players. Now onto the final. First time ever Pakistan had reached the World Cup final after three failed semi-final results. The greatest night of Imran's career. Or would it end in tears? The venue - MCG with more than 1 lakh expected for the final. Against England who were favoured to win. England were playing some sublime cricket led from the front by another of Imran's great rivals, Ian Botham. Imran had lived all his life for this occasion and he deserved to be there.Miandad was impressed. Pakistan had gone through to the final and Imran's decision to keep faith in Inzamam was a masterstroke. Rameez Raja who played in that game and made 44 opening the batting was full of praise for Inzy's batting. "The spark was provided by Inzi, who played the innings of a lifetime, and Javed was the calming partner in that match-winning partnership." Ken Rutherford was awestruck by the impact that Inzamam had on the game. "A lot's been said about the tactics in the field, but I don't go along with that. No one could have foreseen the impact Inzamam would have."Still a lot had to be done to beat New Zealand who had made 262 on a slow surface at the Eden Park in Auckland. Pakistan needed 123 runs in 90 balls when Inzy strode out. His batting form had been poor till then and even Javed Miandad thought he didn't deserve a spot in the team. The critics were out to get him if he failed. Then Inzamam showed his class and maturity as he played one of the all-time great match-turning innings in a high pressure situation. His 37-ball 60 took Pakistan over the winning line. Ironically it was Javed who was with him at the other end as he smashed the Kiwi bowlers all around Eden Park.Legend has it that Inzamam pleaded with Imran to drop him from the team for the semi-final as he had taken ill, but Imran stood firm. Inzy had taken sleeping pills to calm himself before the big semi-final and that move backfired on him. According to ICC-Cricket - "A sleepless night with regular visits to the washroom left him ill and he announced himself unfit on the morning of the match. But Imran, first by himself and then through Aaqib Javed and Mushtaq Ahmed, convinced Inzamam to take the field and bat even if he had to do it in a "wheelchair"."But Pakistan won all three and booked a spot in the semis. There they took on New Zealand and the inspirational leadership of Imran came to the fore again. Young Inzamam was a special talent. Imran knew it. And he believed in him. Inzamam had just 123 runs in eight league games but Imran still believed in him. Any other captain would've dropped a player for such a poor run. But Imran wasn't any other captain. He had the knack of spotting talent and backing them to the hilt. Imran could've taken the easy route and played another batsman in Inzy's spot. He didn't.Pakistan's 1992 campaign got off to a miserable start when they were thumped by West Indies, who won by 10 wickets. Then at Adelaide, they were bowled out for 74 and looked set for a thrashing. But rain came to their rescue and gave them a point - which eventually proved vital as that point took them to the semis. A deflating loss to India was followed by another loss to South Africa. Pakistan's points table showed - played 5, won 1, lost 3, NR 1. There were three more games remaining and all were must-win.
