Don't go away we'll be back with more updates...by Saurabh SomaniWith two wins from two matches, India are in the luxurious position of having the time and space to experiment with different combinations in the coming ODIs against New Zealand - which was the basic idea behind resting the seniors and picking a relatively inexperienced team to start with. That the team has delivered will be of great cheer to fans, but it is important to remember that the series has not yet been won, and the opposition is rather weak. Off the last 8 ODIs that saw a result for them, New Zealand have now lost all 8 and that is a very worrying sign for them. Their problem has always been an inability to produce an all-round good performance. Seldom have all batsmen or all bowlers clicked together, and if by some miracle the batsmen have all clicked the bowlers have let them down and vice versa. Their fans might have believed that the reverse in Bangladesh was an aberration, but based on their results so far, it has seemed an ominous portent rather than an aberration. Also, aberrations occur for a match or at most two matches - not for so many matches in a row. New Zealand can point to the fact that they are missing Brendon McCullum and Jesse Ryder, but with the ultimate goal of the 2011 World Cup in mind, a team has to have the resources to adequately fill the shoes of any missing first-choice members. The World Cup is going to be a long tournament, and an injury or two are almost guaranteed. What New Zealand have shown is that unless they are at full strength, it will be almost impossible for them to consistently string together victories. The primary focus for New Zealand in this and the remaining matches should be to get the players who would be on the bench if their first eleven was fit to become more match-ready. Amongst the best things for the Indian team has been the return of Zaheer Khan. While Zaheer is undoubtedly the prime strike bowler for India in Tests, he hasn't been quite as devastating in ODIs. However, a Zaheer who finds his line and length consistently will still be India's best weapon in ODIs too, so it is essential that he get into the rhythm of sub-continental ODI cricket again with these three ODIs against New Zealand. Since Sreesanth has been rested, Zaheer for Sreesanth becomes one of the straight swaps. Suresh Raina being given a rest means though that Saurabh Tiwary could get a look in, while amongst the bowlers Nehra, Praveen Kumar and Munaf will battle for two spots. The combination could come down to whether Zaheer prefers bowling first change or with the new ball. If Zaheer takes the new ball, Munaf is likely to pip Praveen Kumar into the eleven, since he is a better first change bowler than Praveen is. If, however, Zaheer does not take the new ball then it should be shared between Nehra and Praveen. The combinations from India might change, but the intensity is not likely to.
